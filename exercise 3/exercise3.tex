\section{Analytical calculation of R and L}
First of all, using the inverse of the analytical formulas from before, it is easy to calculate the physical values of the resistance and inductance of the circuit needed to obtain those $f_r$ and $Q$.

\begin{equation}
L = \frac{1}{(2\pi f_R)^2 C} = 30.26\text{ nH} \qquad R = Q\sqrt{\frac{L}{C}} = 6.36\text{ M}\Omega
\end{equation}

The results obtained closely match the theoretical values. Initially, a noticeable discrepancy was observed due to the coarse frequency step used in the simulation, which led to an underestimation of the calculated quantities. By increasing the number of sweep points from 10,000 to 1,000,000, and consequently improving the frequency resolution, the numerical approximation converged more closely to the theoretical predictions, as evidenced by the updated results.
