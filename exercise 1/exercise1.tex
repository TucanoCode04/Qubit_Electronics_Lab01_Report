\section{RLC Parallel Circuit Analytical Analysis}

In this section, the resonance frequency and quality factor of a parallel RLC circuit driven by an AC current source are analytically calculated.

The parameters given in the assignment are reported in Table \ref{tab:parameters}.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{cc}
\hline
Parameter & Value \\
\hline
Current source $I$ & \SI{1}{\ampere} (AC) \\
Inductance $L$ & \SI{30}{\nano\henry} \\
Resistance $R$ & \SI{100}{\kilo\ohm} \\
Capacitance $C$ & \SI{50}{\femto\farad} \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\caption{Circuit parameters}
\label{tab:parameters}
\end{table}

For a parallel RLC circuit the resonance frequency is given by

\begin{equation}
f_R = \frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{LC}}
\end{equation}

while the quality factor is

\begin{equation}
Q = R\sqrt{\frac{C}{L}}
\end{equation}

Substituting the numerical values for the resonance frequency:

\begin{equation}
f_R = \frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{(\SI{30}{\nano\henry})(\SI{50}{\femto\farad})}} \approx 4.109 \ \si{\hertz}
\end{equation}

Similarly, the quality factor becomes

\begin{equation}
Q = (\SI{100}{\kilo\ohm})\sqrt{\frac{\SI{50}{\femto\farad}}{\SI{30}{\nano\henry}}}  \approx 128
\end{equation}

These results provide the theoretical prediction of the ideal resonance behaviour of the circuit. In particular, the resonance frequency indicates that the circuit will exhibit a peak in impedance at approximately \SI{4.109}{\hertz}, while the quality factor indicates that the resonance will be relatively sharp, with a narrow bandwidth around the resonance frequency.

\section{LTspice Simulation}

In order to verify the analytical predictions, the circuit was implemented and simulated using LTspice.

Figure \ref{fig:ltspice} shows the schematic representation of the parallel RLC circuit as implemented in LTspice.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.7\linewidth]{./exercise 1/RLCParallelCircuit.png}
\caption{Parallel RLC circuit implemented in LTspice}
\label{fig:ltspice}
\end{figure}

The AC analysis was performed using the following simulation directive:

\begin{lstlisting}
.ac dec 1000 1 20G
\end{lstlisting}

In frequency domain analysis, the impedance \(Z\) of the circuit can be calculated as the ratio of the voltage \(V\) across the circuit to the current \(I\) supplied by the source:
\begin{equation}
Z = \frac{V}{I}  = V = 99.98 \ \si{\kilo\ohm}
\end{equation}

Since the current source is set to \SI{1}{\ampere}, the voltage across the circuit directly corresponds to the impedance.
\subsection{Resonance Frequency}

The resonance frequency corresponds to the frequency at which the impedance reaches its maximum value. 
This quantity can be extracted in LTspice using the following directive:

\begin{lstlisting}
.meas AC max_v MAX mag(V(n001))
.meas AC f_res WHEN mag(V(n001))=max_v
\end{lstlisting}

The simulated resonance frequency was found to be approximately \SI{4.109}{\hertz}, which is in excellent agreement with the analytical prediction obtained in the previous section.
\subsection{Bandwidth}

The bandwidth of the resonance can be determined by finding the frequencies at which the impedance drops to
\begin{equation}
\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}
\end{equation}

of the maximum value, which corresponds to the -3 dB points of the resonance curve.

The following commands were used:

\begin{lstlisting}
.meas AC v_3db PARAM max_v*0.707
.meas AC f_low WHEN mag(V(n001))=v_3db RISE=1
.meas AC f_high WHEN mag(V(n001))=v_3db FALL=1
\end{lstlisting}

The results of the simulation indicate that the lower and upper -3 dB frequencies are approximately $f_1 \approx \SI{4.093}{\giga\hertz}$ and $f_2 \approx \SI{4.125}{\giga\hertz}$, respectively.

The bandwidth is then calculated as

\begin{equation}
\Delta f = f_2 - f_1 \approx 31.8 \ \si{M\hertz}
\end{equation}
\subsection{Quality Factor}

Using the simulated values of the resonance frequency and the bandwidth, the quality factor can be estimated as

\begin{equation}
Q = \frac{f_R}{\Delta f} \approx 128.809
\end{equation}

This value is in very good agreement with the analytical prediction of \(Q \approx 128\), confirming the consistency between the theoretical analysis and the simulation results.
